CS Jobs
There are as many jobs in Computer Science as there are fields. Here are a few examples of what kind of jobs exist.
Software Dev at Intel
Software engineers at Intel focus on building and optimizing software that powers Intel’s hardware, including drivers, firmware, and development tools. The work blends coding with hardware integration, requiring strong problem-solving and collaboration across engineering teams.
Typical salaries range from $95k–$140k at mid-level, with entry-level roles starting around $80k–$90k and senior positions reaching $150k–$190k+.
Web Dev at Apple
Web developers at Apple create and maintain the web experiences that support Apple’s products, services, and online platforms. They focus on building clean, responsive, and secure interfaces, optimizing performance at scale, and collaborating with designers and engineers to deliver seamless user experiences.
At Apple, web developers typically earn $100k–$140k at mid-level, with entry-level positions starting around $85k–$95k and senior roles often reaching $150k–$180k+, depending on experience and location.
U.S. Army Cyber Program
Cybersecurity specialists in the U.S. Army Cyber Command safeguard military networks and critical infrastructure against cyber threats. Their responsibilities include detecting and neutralizing intrusions, conducting vulnerability assessments, supporting offensive cyber operations, and ensuring the resilience of command-and-control systems. Specialists often work alongside intelligence and signal units to protect classified information and mission readiness.
Salaries typically range from $55k–$95k, depending on rank, years of service, and certifications, with additional allowances and benefits unique to military service.
Epic Games Dev
Game developers at Epic Games design, build, and maintain interactive experiences using Unreal Engine and other proprietary tools. They work on gameplay systems, graphics, physics, and online features, collaborating closely with designers, artists, and engineers to bring games and virtual worlds to life.
At Epic Games, developers typically earn $95k–$150k at mid-level, with entry-level roles starting around $80k–$95k and senior positions often reaching $160k–$200k+, depending on experience and location.
