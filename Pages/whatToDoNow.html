What To Do Now?
Computer Science (CS) is more than just coding — it’s about solving problems, building cool things, and shaping the future. And the best part? You don’t need to wait for college to get started. Whether you're a teenager curious about how your favorite apps work, or a young adult exploring a new passion, CS is something you can start learning right now — no expensive tools or fancy degrees needed.
1. Learn the Basics of Coding
Learn a beginner-friendly language like Python or try Scratch if you're completely new. These will help you understand how computers think, and get your problem solving skills moving
2. Build Something Small
Don’t wait to be an expert — build as you learn. A simple game, calculator, or website is a great place to start. As long as you are coding you are training your CS skill.
3. Find a Community
Join a school coding club, participate in hackathons, or connect with others online. Learning is easier (and more fun) with others. Do not be scared to ask for help!
